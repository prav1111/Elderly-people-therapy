India faces a growing challenge in elderly care as its senior population rises sharply, projected to reach 19% by 2050. Many elderly individuals, especially in rural areas, live alone or in old age homes, struggling with social isolation, loneliness, and mental health issues like depression. Although government schemes exist, support structures remain inadequate, leaving many seniors vulnerable. To address these emotional and psychological challenges, innovative solutions such as AI-driven conversational agents could provide companionship and mental stimulation, helping to improve the quality of life for elderly citizens. Urgent action is needed to support this rapidly growing demographic.
Many elderly individuals face mental health challenges, with depression being especially common. The World Health Organization estimates that 10% to 20% of older adults suffer from depressive disorders. Rates are higher among those who have lost their partners and live alone, as loneliness and grief can significantly impact emotional well-being.

Despite this, many seniors do not seek help due to stigma or the belief that depression is a normal part of aging. Raising awareness, encouraging social interaction, and providing access to mental health care are essential steps to support the elderly and improve their overall quality of life.
